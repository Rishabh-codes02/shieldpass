Cybersecurity Essentials

  • Avoid Pattern Re-use Never recycle identical passwords across multiple online profiles. If one platform undergoes a data breach, all your linked credentials fall instantly.
  • Beware of Social Engineering Attackers often guess passwords based on easily public data like your birth year, hometown, pet names, or family members.
  • Enforce Multi-Factor Authentication (MFA) Even an unbroken, robust password requires an extra buffer line. Enable authenticator tokens or biometrics wherever possible.
  • Monitor Device Environments Keep browsers and operating software fully updated to patch key vulnerabilities that malware could use to intercept data payloads.
